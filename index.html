WHERE TO WORK ?

A recent survey conducted by Employment Hero revealed that a staggering 72% of Malaysians express a desire to work abroad. Their motivation stems from factors such as a weak exchange rate, inadequate compensation, and extended working hours in Malaysia. In this visualization, our aim is to delve into the working conditions and opportunities available in various countries, shedding light on the factors that drive individuals to seek employment overseas.

World GDP 2023

GDP is the financial pulse of a nation, offering a vital peek into its economic vitality and overall prosperity. It's not just a number; it's a window into the well-being of a country. Higher GDP per capita signifies a robust economy with ample job opportunities, akin to a bustling job marketplace.

High GDP in North America, Western Europe, and Oceania stems from strong institutions, an educated workforce, modern capital, and entrepreneurial vigor. Western countries, with affordable education for the masses, stable politics, and inviting policies, attract investors and talent, leading to robust economies and high GDP figures.

Total Tax Rates 2023

The total tax rate, a percentage of commercial profits, impacts people by squeezing businesses' ability to pay better wages, potentially stunting job oppurtunities, and causing consumers to foot the bill through higher prices.As such,expat will have less job oppurtunities

Asia has the lowest total tax rate following by Europe.

Qatar, UAE, Singapore, and Luxembourg boast the lowest tax rates in their respective continents, making them magnets for expats. High income and low taxes are the magic combo here. These nations rely on foreign workers and investments, so they keep taxes low to keep their economic engine running. They're top picks for expat work!

Average Salary,Work Life Balance and Life Satisfaction

While economic factors are important, they're not the only indicator.As an expat, you'll want to know if you'll be able to enjoy your life in your new home so you will have motivation to work long term.

In the big picture, higher salaries seem to bring more life satisfaction. Northern Europe, Western Europe, and Western countries top the charts with both great salaries and contentment. If you seek work-life balance and aren't too concerned about taxes, Europe shines as the ideal destination.
