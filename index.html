Where to Work Abroad ?

A recent survey conducted by Employment Hero revealed that a staggering 72% of Malaysians express a desire to work abroad. Their motivation stems from factors such as a weak exchange rate, inadequate compensation, and extended working hours in Malaysia. In this visualization, our aim is to delve into the working conditions and opportunities available in various countries, shedding light on the factors that drive individuals to seek employment overseas.

The choropleth map shown above illustrates the GDP per capita across the world in 2022. By having a careful look at the map, we can see that the countries in the America continent has the highest GDP per capita while many countries in the Africa continent seems like there are not many countries with a relatively high GDP per capita since the color depicted on the continent is mostly the bright color.

We'll also explore life satisfaction, work-life balance indices, and average wages across various occupations in each country. The scatter plot above illustrates the correlation between life satisfaction and work-life balance indices in different nations. Notably, countries with higher average wages tend to exhibit elevated levels of both life satisfaction and work-life balance. This pattern suggests that Europe stands out as an appealing destination for international employment.
